こんにちは

Nihongo Notes is personal collection of my knowledge on Japanese language, which isn't much. but I will be trying my best update the contents along the way. Feel free to peruse this poorly written "website" ;).

Japanese alphabets are divided into three types. Hiragana, Katakana and Kanji. Hiragana are native Japanese alphabets, they don't have meaning in on themeselves and are used to write native words. Katakana are phonetically similar to Hiraganas but are written differently. They are used to write loan words. Kanji are characters taken from the Chinese script and used in the writing of Japanese. A single kanji represents an idea/meaning as a whole. They are often derived from pictures or painting of the thing they represent.

Hiragana

Hiragana consists of 46 different characters which are shown in table below. The hiragana in top row are 5 vowel sound and are prononced a little different from what their romaji represent:

  • a as in "car"
  • i as in "ink"
  • u as in "super"
  • e as in "ten"
  • o as in "rope"
And on the left, the consonants are listed k, s, t, n, h, m, y, r, w, n. Which together mixed with the vowels gives them a sound. Note that these hiragana have to be written with a certain stroke order, which I have not included because of my laziness.
