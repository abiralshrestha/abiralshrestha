Kana

Japanese alphabets are divided into three types.

Hiragana, Katakana and Kanji. Hiragana are native Japanese alphabets, they don't have meaning in on themeselves and are used to write native words. Katakana are phonetically similar to Hiraganas but are written differently. They are used to write loan words. Kanji are characters borrowed from the Chinese language. A single kanji represents an idea/meaning as a whole. They are often derived from pictures or painting of the thing they represent.
